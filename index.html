Welcome to Assam House Restaurant

A unique fusion of old and new, Ipoh is a town filled with wild tales of yesteryear, and presents a colourful history to her visitors. The early Chinese immigrants who came from the southern Fujian province in China, brought their cooking style into the state.

These traditional cooking styles blended well with the local spices, which started a new breed of savoury delicacies. Assam House dedicates itself to serving splendid gastronomical Asian fare with exceptional service, and has succeeded branding itself as simply the best Asian cuisine in Malaysia.

History of Ipoh

The olden Ipoh days were bustling with activities. One can imagine the Chinapeks were busy with their daily business, rushing about in their trustly old bicycles. Housewives chatting among each other as they tended to everyday's chores. Despite its Asian setting, British architecture of the colonial era stood proudly till today, a testimony to the British's long and illustrious rule. Over the years, Ipoh endured many changes but maintained its distinct identity.

Did you know? Hale Lane, Panglima Lane and Market Lane in Ipoh are known respectively in Cantonese dialect as Fist Concubine's Lane, Second and Third Concubine's Lane. History has it that before the Japanese Occupation, the three lines lad their share of brothels, gaming house and opium dens.
